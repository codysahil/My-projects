   Process:

The clay and sand mixture is then moulded into brick shapes using a brick mould. This process can be done either manually or by using a machine.The moulded bricks are then left to dry in the sun for several days. During this time, the bricks lose moisture and become hard enough to withstand handling. The dried bricks are then burned in a kiln at high temperatures to make them stronger and more durable. The temperature and duration of the burning process is carefully controlled to produce the desired quality of brick.
